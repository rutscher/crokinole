A. Earth Tones

B. Wood Species Gradient

Light to dark wood tones — birch through ebony. Each ring is a different "species." Totally cohesive with the board, but might be too subtle to quickly differentiate at a glance during gameplay.

C. Same Disc, Different Size

All discs are the same natural wood color. Value communicated through size (bigger = more points) and a subtle colored ring border. Feels most like real crokinole discs — but the color hint keeps them scannable.

D. Wood-Stained Discs

Same green/blue/red/gold mapping but as wood stain colors — like the dye soaked into wood. Keeps the color-coding familiar but everything looks like it belongs on the board. The wood grain "shows through" the color.

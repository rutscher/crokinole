Progress Bar Styles

Same layout (B), different bar visual treatments. All show P1 at 42% and P2 at 37%.

End Round

1: Thin Flat

5px bars, solid gradient fill. Minimal and clean.

End Round

2: Chunky + Glow

10px bars with inner glow and subtle border. More visible, more presence.

End Round

3: Segmented

10 blocks = 10 points each. Partially filled block for remainder. Easy to read at a glance — "4 blocks filled = about 40".

End Round

4: Opposing

Single bar — P1 (gold) grows from left, P2 (steel) from right. When they meet in the middle, someone's close to winning. Compact — just one bar.

End Round

5: Arc Gauges

Semi-circle gauges, one per player. Compact, readable from both orientations since they're symmetrical shapes. Gaming feel.

My recommendation: 3 (Segmented) — easiest to read at a glance from either orientation. The discrete blocks make it obvious how close someone is to winning without needing to read numbers. But 4 (Opposing) is a close second for its compactness and the drama of the bars racing toward each other.

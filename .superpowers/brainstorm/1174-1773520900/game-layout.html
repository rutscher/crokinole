A: Split Screen

Each player gets half the screen with their own score and tap zones. Like a chess clock — each side "belongs" to a player. Rotated so each player can read their half from their side of the board.

B: Unified Scoreboard

Scores side-by-side at top, shared scoring zone below. One person operates it (or pass it back and forth). Tab between players to enter their discs. More like a traditional scoreboard.

C: Swipe Focus

One player fills the screen at a time with big, easy-to-tap buttons. Swipe to switch between players. Compact score bar at top keeps both scores visible. Most touch-friendly for phone use.
